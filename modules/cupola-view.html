The Cupola Module

The Cupola is a seven-windowed observatory module on the ISS, installed in 2010. It features six side windows and one top window, each 20 inches (51 cm) in diameter. This is where astronauts take the breathtaking photos of Earth you see online!

👁️

Window to the World

From the Cupola, astronauts can observe 90% of Earth's surface. The station orbits at 408 km altitude, completing one orbit every 90 minutes. That means 16 sunrises and sunsets every single day!

📸

Photography & Science

Astronauts use the Cupola to photograph hurricanes, wildfires, and geographic features for scientific research. They also control the Canadarm2 robotic arm from here, monitoring spacewalks and cargo operations.

📏

🚀

⏱️

Astronauts see 16 sunrises and 16 sunsets daily

🌍

🤖

📸

Source of most iconic Earth photos from space
